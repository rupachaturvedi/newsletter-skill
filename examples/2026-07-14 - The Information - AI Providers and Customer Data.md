# Are AI Providers Really a Threat to Their Customers' Data?

**Source:** The Information, Applied AI (Aaron Holmes)
**Date:** July 14, 2026
**Link:**

## Summary

Rivals of the big AI labs, including the CEOs of Microsoft, Salesforce, and Palantir, are warning that AI providers can use customer data to build competing products. The piece argues the real risk is subtler than models training on prompts: the high-level usage data providers collect, plus a loophole where individuals voluntarily share their chats depending on privacy settings. Both Anthropic and OpenAI say they do not train on enterprise prompts, though they use some individual chat logs depending on user privacy choices.

## Why it matters

Enterprise data trust is turning into a competitive wedge that incumbent software vendors use against the AI labs. Relevant to how AI product teams frame data handling and privacy defaults.
